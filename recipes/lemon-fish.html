An Exquisite Pan Seared Lemon Herb Fish

I love seafood, it's 'shrimply' the best.

Pan Seared Fish: This fish is so good, it could make a grown man weep. It's crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and so flavorful you'd consider living in the ocean. But seriously, this pan-seared fish is so delicious, it might just be the catch of the day, every day.

Ingredients

  • 4 fish fillets (such as trout, snapper, or salmon)
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, thyme, or dill)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps:

It's time to get your 'roe' on!

  1. Rinse the fish fillets and pat them dry with a paper towel. Season both sides of the fish with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the butter and olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Once the butter is melted and the oil is hot, add the fish fillets to the skillet. Sear the fish on one side for about 3-4 minutes, until the skin is golden brown and crispy.
  4. Flip the fish over and add the minced garlic to the skillet. Sear the fish on the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes.
  5. Remove the fish from the skillet and place it on a plate.
  6. To the same skillet, add the freshly squeezed lemon juice and chopped fresh herbs. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the herbs are fragrant and the sauce has slightly thickened.
  7. Pour the herb and lemon sauce over the fish fillets.
  8. Serve the pan-seared lemon herb fish with your choice of sides, such as steamed vegetables or rice.
